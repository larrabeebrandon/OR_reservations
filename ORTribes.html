The Nine Federally Recognized Tribes

There are nine federally recognized Tribes in the State of Oregon. The reconized tribes include
  • Burns Paiute Tribe
  • Confederated Tribs of the Coos, Lower Umpqua and Siuslaw Indians
  • Confederated Tribes of the Grand Ronde Community of Oregon
  • Confederated Tribes of Siletz Indians
  • Confederated Tribes of the Umatilla Indian Reservation
  • Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs
  • Coquille Indian Tribe
  • Cow Creek Band of Umpqua Tribe of Indians
  • Klamath Tribes
Most of the Tribes are confederations which means that the within each confederation there are many different bands and smaller tribes that were grouped together to form one large tribe. These confederates were granted lands known as reservations which were granted to the Tribes in exchange for their homeland. Every Oregon Tribe experienced forced removal by the federal government in order to steal the resource rich land of a disenfranchised people.
