The Nine Federally Recognized Tribes

There are nine federally recognized Tribes in the State of Oregon. The reconized tribes include
  • Burns Paiute Tribe
  • Confederated Tribs of the Coos, Lower Umpqua and Siuslaw Indians
  • Confederated Tribes of the Grand Ronde Community of Oregon
  • Confederated Tribes of Siletz Indians
  • Confederated Tribes of the Umatilla Indian Reservation
  • Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs
  • Coquille Indian Tribe
  • Cow Creek Band of Umpqua Tribe of Indians
  • Klamath Tribes
Most of the Tribes are confederations which means that the within each confederation there are many different bands and smaller tribes that were grouped together to form one large tribe. These confederates were granted lands known as reservations which were granted to the Tribes in exchange for their homeland. Every Oregon Tribe experienced forced removal by the federal government in order to steal the resource rich land of a disenfranchised people. The Siletz Reservation

The original reservation set aside for the Siletz people by President Pierce on November 9, 1855 was 1.1 million acres and was traded for approximately 19 million acres that the tribes originally inhabited. After the people were relocated to the Siletz reservation the treaties were ignored by the americans and by 1875 more than 900,000 acres had been taken from the Siletz without regard to treaties already in place. The 1887 Allotment Act was a further attack on the land holdings of the Siletz by parceling out what was left to living tribal members they were able to take the rest of the land because the living members had already been alloted their parcel. The rest of the land was taken and now is what is known as Lincoln County.>
